Filipino Pork Adobo

Marinated Pork

The word 'Adobo' is derived from the Spanish word 'Adobar', meaning to marinate through the use of a liquid mix, or through a rub using powdered ingredients. The Filipino version of Adobo suggests marinating the pork in soy sauce and crushed garlic. Then cooked with vinegar.

What you will need:

  • Pork Belly
  • Garlic
  • White Vinegar
  • Soy Sauce
  • Peppercorn
  • Onion
  • Dried Bay Leaves
  • A container to store your marinated pork belly
  • A cooking pot

How to make it:

  1. Cut the pork belly to your preferred size and crush garlic cloves
  2. Mix the cut pork belly and the crushed garlic into a container. Add in soy sauce to your preference, add less for a drier adobo.
  3. Let it marinade for at least one hour. If you have plenty of time, marinate it overnight for stronger flavour to penetrate the pork
  4. Place your marinated pork in a cooking pot, cook until dark brown but not burnt. Keep the marinade sauce and garlic for later
  5. Once sufficiently browned, pour in the remaining marinade sauce and garlic into the pot
  6. Add about 500 ml of water (add less for a drier adobo), peppercorns, and dried bay leaves then bring to a boil. Simmer for about 40 minutes to 1 hour.
  7. Put in about four tablespoons of vinegar and let it simmer for another 12 to 15 minutes
  8. Add salt to taste
  9. Serve hot with rice and enjoy!
